Ubicity Demo Application


This demonstrator shows how social media indexing can deliver new knowledge. Ubicity has indexed hundreds of millions of geo-referenced tweets, which you can search by typing one or more search terms in the search box. Any results will be shown on their location of origin – or in their region of origin – on the map. When not zoomed in, only result counts will appear, attached to a geographic bounding box. Zooming in will show ever more detailed counts, until individual tweets are shown.
The amount of tweets per time is depicted on a timeline diagram. Zooming in and out of this diagram using the mouse wheel will scale the timeline to days, half days, 6-hour and 3-hour intervals. The timeline can be shifted into the past by dragging on the diagram using the left mouse button.
A Wikipedia dump has also been indexed by Ubicity. Pages containing the search term in the page title are displayed in a circle graph together with the titles of Wikipedia pages linked from these. Selecting any node in the circle graph will set the search term to the node text and a new query will be performed.
Finally, Flickr images which are tagged with the search term are indexed on demand and shown as a slide show on the screen.
